# Prompt Engineering Wiki

A friendly, searchable knowledge base for everyone learning to talk to language models better.

## What it is

Prompt Engineering Wiki collects the patterns, techniques, and hard-won lessons of working with AI language models into one approachable library. It treats prompting as a craft — explaining concepts like role prompting, chain-of-thought, few-shot examples, structured outputs, and context windows in plain language with real examples.

Whether the reader is writing their first ChatGPT prompt or their fifty-thousandth, the wiki aims to be the resource they bookmark, share, and come back to when they're stuck.

## Who it's for

- **Beginners** who just got access to an AI tool and want to use it well.
- **Developers** integrating language models into products who need reliable prompting patterns.
- **Writers, marketers, and creators** improving AI-assisted drafts.
- **Educators and team leads** training others on effective AI usage.
- **Researchers and power users** who want a central place to reference advanced techniques.

## Use cases

- Look up a specific technique ("how do I get the model to output JSON?").
- Browse the full knowledge base to level up broadly.
- Read deep-dive articles on individual topics with examples.
- Share links to specific techniques with teammates or students.
- Submit feedback, corrections, or suggestions through the contact flow.

## Core capabilities

- **Knowledge base browser** for exploring articles by topic and difficulty.
- **Individual article pages** with clear explanations, examples, and takeaways.
- **Curated resources section** pointing to further reading and external tools.
- **Search and navigation** that makes it easy to find a specific idea fast.
- **About, FAQ, and Contact pages** for context, common questions, and reaching the authors.

## The idea in one line

Prompt Engineering Wiki turns the informal folklore of prompting into a calm, well-organized library anyone can learn from.
